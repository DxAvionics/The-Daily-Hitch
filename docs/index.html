◈ HITCHENS QUESTION OF THE DAY
When the Pentagon warns of AI exploitation while simultaneously funding robotic killing machines for Ukrainian battlefields, and Congress debates the proper civilian role of military lawyers while drone swarms buzz through Middle Eastern shipping lanes, one might reasonably ask: exactly which century's moral framework are we pretending still governs the machinery of state violence? The same defense establishment that worries about "safeguarding models" seems remarkably cavalier about safeguarding the principle that humans, not algorithms, should retain the final decision over who lives and dies. Or have we already conceded that particular point while arguing over the paperwork?
