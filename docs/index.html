◈ HITCHENS QUESTION OF THE DAY
When did we decide that the systematic erasure of entire villages—whether Rohingya in Myanmar, Lebanese in the south, or any other inconvenient population—merits only the polite euphemism of "lost" settlements while we simultaneously spend ten billion dollars debating the organizational chart of our cyber warriors? Perhaps the real question isn't whether we need a separate Cyber Force, but whether we've already surrendered our moral force so completely that we can read about deliberate demographic engineering in the morning headlines with the same detached interest we reserve for Pentagon contract announcements. Is this what victory in the "rules-based international order" looks like—a world where satellite imagery captures atrocities in real-time while we haggle over the bureaucratic structure of our digital defenses?
